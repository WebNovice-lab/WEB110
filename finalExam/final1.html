I Love Jan Karon's Mitford Book Series

final1     final2     final3     final4    

In the late 1990's I discovered my favorite author, Jan Karon, and her beloved Mitford series through a free subscription to Victoria Magazine.

Jan was the writer in residence at the time.

An excerpt from her current book would be published in each month's edition and I would devour them and the recipes they featured. I became so enamored with the series that I sought out the books at the public library and, to my delight, discovered the audio versions on cassette tape.

My four sons and I began listening to the narrator, John McDonough, bring the characters to life with different accents-mostly southern-and colorful personalities. Mitford became a home away from home and a source of fun discussions in our homeschool.

My sons are all adults now with children of their own, but we still quote the characters of Mitford, especially Uncle Billy who counted it his duty to make people laugh.

Mitford is now my "happy place" when life gets to be too heavy and serious. It's now possible to listen to any of the books on demand; however, for nostalgia's sake, I regularly check out the CD recordings of the books from the library and listen during my commute. A good belly laugh from Mitford is a wonderful way to cope with rush hour traffic.
